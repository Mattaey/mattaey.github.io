Copying Lyrical Metadata

June 13, 2019

I never find myself too interested in the intellectual property cases that occur outside of the gaming industry, but, a Variety article titled "Genius Accuses Google of Scraping Song Lyrics, Music Data Company Hits Back" caught my eye this week.

It is a weird situation that I've thought about before, especially when using Spotify. Sometimes, when listening to my playlists on Spotify's app, the album artwork rotates, and I can see lyrics and artists' anecdotes. This is all provided by Genius, a popular lyric website, and I assumed the artists’ and their labels gave permission to the website.

Yet, apparently, the industry doesn't normally operate like this. Google partners with a lyric publisher (who obtains the metadata as a third-party contractor) with much less scrutiny.

Which is fine, not every industry needs to be regulated to death, but I'm curious about the accountability. Is it even possible to sue Google (as Genius), if their third-party is using your metadata? If they obtain the rights to the lyrics of, say, Like a Rolling Stone, and publish with your metadata, is there a course of action?

They have the rights to the lyrics, and there's no tangible difference between your metadata and the original work they have the rights to. To me, this seems like a rare case of data being evaluated at more than its worth. Never thought I'd say that sentence in 2019.
